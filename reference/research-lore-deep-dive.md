# Fallout 76: Comprehensive Lore Deep Dive

**Purpose:** Knowledge base for recognizing anomalies in game files. Covers all major storylines, factions, cryptids, vaults, and environmental storytelling from launch (2018) through The Backwoods update (March 2026).

**Game Timeline:** Fallout 76 begins in 2102, exactly 25 years after the Great War (October 23, 2077). It is the earliest entry in the Fallout timeline. The game is set in Appalachia, covering pre-war West Virginia and parts of Virginia and Ohio.

## 1. Pre-War West Virginia

### Geography and Economy

Appalachia encompasses the pre-war state of West Virginia and parts of Virginia and Ohio. The Appalachian Mountains were a popular vacation destination, supporting a robust tourism industry with ski resorts, bed-and-breakfasts, cabins, and hiking trails. The region was divided into six distinct zones:

- **The Forest** -- the relatively lush, least-irradiated starting zone around Vault 76
- **Toxic Valley** -- heavily polluted industrial wasteland in the north
- **The Ash Heap** -- coal mining country, choked with ash and smog from perpetually burning mines
- **The Savage Divide** -- mountainous spine running through the center
- **The Mire** -- overgrown swampland in the east, transformed by Vault 94's G.E.C.K. meltdown
- **Cranberry Bog** -- irradiated wetlands in the southeast, epicenter of Scorchbeast activity

### Industrial Powers

Two corporations dominated pre-war Appalachian industry:

**Hornwright Industrial Mining Company** -- Automated mining giant that developed Auto-Miners and the Motherlode drilling machine. Ruthlessly pursued automation, displacing human miners and causing massive unemployment and protests. Their corporate headquarters in Charleston was a symbol of corporate excess.

**Garrahan Mining Company** -- The human-labor mining company that competed against Hornwright. Developed the EX-17 Excavator Power Armor under Harold Frost, siphoned from U.S. Army Research Corps in early 2075. Used black titanium (native to Appalachia) for its tensile strength and an ultracite reactor for power. Completed February 2077. Hornwright sabotaged a public mining competition, leading to Garrahan's stock collapse and a hostile takeover by Hornwright.

**Atomic Mining Services (AMS)** -- Operated mining complexes throughout Appalachia. Their abandoned mines later became sites for Enclave scorchbeast experiments. Held patents on ultracite ore processing.

### Vault-Tec University

Located in Morgantown. In 2031, Vault-Tec rebranded Morgantown's local college as Vault-Tec University. Over 46 years, the company stripped classical education programs and transformed the school into a vocational training center for Vault-Tec employees. Most professors were fired; only those teaching company-relevant subjects were retained.

The university featured a simulation vault underground where Overseer candidates carried out proposed experiments. The final experiment (October 15, 2077) by Drew Collingsworth tested an artificial food paste, but Dean Elliot secretly altered it to induce lethal arterial plaque buildup to observe dweller reactions to unsafe food supplies. The Great War occurred during this experiment (October 23, 2077), and the simulation vault continued operating with lethal consequences.

A secret wing of VTU housed meetings between Vault-Tec executives, military delegates, and government officials. This wing later proved critical to learning about Vault 79's gold reserves.

### Military Installations

The remoteness of West Virginia attracted extensive military investment:

- **Sugar Grove** -- Naval Radio Station and NSA SIGINT blacksite. Monitored miners for seditious sentiments, investigated money trails for Chinese connections, tracked secessionists. Housed Project SIPHON (CX-15), an automated data exfiltration holotape system using iridium-infused magnetic tapes ($15 million per holotape).
- **The National Isolated Radio Array (NIRA)** -- Communications installation
- **Missile Silos Alpha, Bravo, and Charlie** -- Part of the Appalachian Automated Launch System, nuclear ICBM silos that survived the Great War and remained operational
- **Camp McClintock** -- U.S. Army basic training facility
- **Fort Defiance** (formerly Allegheny Asylum) -- later commandeered by the Brotherhood of Steel

### West Tek Research Center

West Tek operated a research facility near Huntersville where they conducted Forced Evolutionary Virus (FEV) experiments. As early as February 2076, FEV samples were released into Huntersville's water supply after the Greenhouse project was shuttered and assets redirected to the Super Mutant project. This created Appalachia's super mutant population. Failed FEV experiments also produced the Snallygaster (strain FEVS-006443, October 14, 2077) and the Grafton Monster (strain FEVS-006458, October 23, 2077).

### Social Unrest

Pre-war Appalachia was marked by stark inequality. Automation displaced workers, corporate overreach was rampant, and resource shortages bred desperation. Massive protests and riots erupted, culminating in the labor unrest that the Free States movement would later exploit. The contrast between Hornwright's gleaming automated mines and the poverty of displaced miners is a recurring theme in environmental storytelling throughout the game.

## 2. The Scorched Plague and Scorchbeasts

### Origins (Early 2083)

The Scorchbeasts were accidentally created when Enclave scientists, under Secretary Thomas Eckhart's orders, exposed irradiated bats to biochemical experiments in early 2083. The experiments were conducted in abandoned AMS mining complexes. The resulting mutation produced gigantic, ferocious flying predators that secreted a virulent mutagenic pathogen -- the Scorched Plague.

### The Plague Mechanism

The Scorched Plague is spread through naturally-secreted particulate falling from Scorchbeast wings. When a living creature is infected:

1. Skin undergoes rapid change from charred black to blood-red lesions (appearing flayed alive)
2. Complete hair loss and eye cataracts develop
3. Green ultracite crystals begin protruding randomly from the skin
4. The subject loses individual will and becomes part of a hive mind controlled by the Scorchbeast Queen
5. Infected humans become "Scorched" -- hostile entities that can still use weapons and speak in fragmented, garbled sentences
6. Wildlife can also be infected, creating Scorched variants of standard creatures

### Ultracite Connection

Ultracite is a highly energetic crystalline mineral native to Appalachia, particularly concentrated in the Cranberry Bog region. It serves as a power source and has mutagenic properties. The Scorchbeast biology is intrinsically linked to ultracite -- the crystals growing from Scorched victims are ultracite formations, and the creatures nest in ultracite-rich underground caverns.

### Timeline of the Plague

- **Early 2083** -- First Scorchbeasts created in Enclave experiments
- **June 2086** -- Eckhart deliberately releases Scorchbeasts onto Appalachia's surface to raise DEFCON levels, enabling nuclear silo access
- **2086** -- Harpers Ferry falls to Scorchbeasts; Free States retreat deeper into the Mire
- **January 29, 2095** -- Brotherhood launches Operation Touchdown in the Glassed Cavern; Paladin Taggerdy dies
- **August 18, 2095** -- Last Brotherhood defenders at Fort Defiance are overrun
- **September-November 2096** -- Responders collapse under combined Scorched and raider pressure
- **2102** -- Vault 76 opens; dwellers begin fighting the Scorched
- **2103** -- Vault 76 dwellers cull significant Scorchbeast populations, produce and distribute inoculation

### The Scorchbeast Queen

The Queen serves as the apex predator and hive mind controller of the Scorched network. She resides deep underground and can be summoned by launching a nuclear strike at a fissure site in the Cranberry Bog. Killing the Queen disrupts the hive mind's control over nearby Scorched.

### Inoculation

By 2103, through combined efforts of Vault 76 dwellers and knowledge recovered from the Responders' incomplete vaccine research at AVR Medical Center, an inoculation against the Scorched Plague was developed and mass-distributed. This prevented further transmission to organized human groups returning to Appalachia.

## 3. The Overseer's Story

### Background

The Overseer of Vault 76 (never given a canonical first name in the original game, later revealed as a fully-voiced NPC in Wastelanders) was the only child of a couple from Sutton, West Virginia. Her mother was killed in a mining accident, after which the young Overseer withdrew into the library. Her childhood friend Evan brought her lunch daily after his shifts.

### Vault-Tec Career

At a career fair in Morgantown, she met the Vault-Tec recruitment team and described the experience as transformative -- "like a light turned on." She dedicated herself to protecting families and America's future. After four years at Vault-Tec University (earning a degree in nuclear fusion with honors in the Overseer track) and a field posting at the Vault-Tec Agricultural Research Center, she was fast-tracked for an Overseer position, skipping years of waiting.

### The Dark Truth

She learned the truth about the Societal Preservation Program -- that Vault-Tec's vaults were experiments on their own dwellers. While she recognized the ethical wrongness, she justified it as necessary for humanity's survival. Rather than retaliating against her for discovering the truth, Vault-Tec took her into their confidence.

### Personal Sacrifice

The Overseer was originally slated for Vault 101 (in Washington, D.C.) due to her engagement with Evan. However, she could only remain Overseer of Vault 76 in Appalachia as an unmarried woman. She broke off her engagement with Evan, leaving him on the surface when the bombs fell.

### The Mission (2102)

After watching over her Vault family for 25 years, the Overseer left Vault 76 before Reclamation Day on a secret Vault-Tec mission: secure the three active nuclear ICBM silos (Site Alpha, Site Bravo, Site Charlie) in Appalachia. She left care packages and holotape recordings along her journey for her dwellers to find.

### Evan's Fate

When she returned to the mines on Mount Blair to see if Evan survived underground, she discovered he had been infected by the Scorched Plague and transformed into a Scorched. Unable to mercy-kill him herself, she locked him away and asked her Vault dwellers to do it instead, using his fate to steel her resolve against the Scorchbeast threat.

### Wastelanders Era (2103)

The Overseer re-emerged as a fully voiced NPC, uniting with the Vault 76 dwellers. Her goals shifted to:
1. Developing and distributing a widespread Scorched Plague vaccine
2. Establishing a gold bullion-based currency for Appalachia's future
3. Investigating Vault 79 through the secret wing of Vault-Tec University
4. Discovering that Vault 79 housed the entire Fort Knox gold reserve

## 4. Brotherhood of Steel in Appalachia

### Taggerdy's Thunder (2077-2095)

**Lieutenant Elizabeth Taggerdy** commanded a U.S. Army Ranger unit relocated to Appalachia for war games against Marine detachments after extended foreign deployment. When the bombs fell, Taggerdy and her unit survived at Camp Venture, a former Free States survival training camp.

**Roger Maxson**, founder of the Brotherhood of Steel in California, contacted Taggerdy via satellite radio. Recognizing the depression and purposelessness threatening to consume his scattered contacts, Maxson offered Taggerdy and her unit a new identity as the Appalachian chapter of the Brotherhood of Steel. Taggerdy became Paladin Taggerdy.

### Doctrine and Conflict

Taggerdy only recruited from those with military experience, creating friction with civilian factions like the Responders. The Brotherhood established Fort Defiance (formerly Allegheny Asylum) as their Appalachian headquarters and focused on recovering and securing pre-war technology, particularly weaponry.

### The Ultracite Discovery

The Brotherhood discovered ultracite in Appalachia and recognized its potential as a power source for weapons and armor. This discovery drove much of their later operations in the Cranberry Bog region, where ultracite deposits were concentrated.

### Operation Touchdown (January 29, 2095)

Paladin Taggerdy led the Brotherhood's most elite troops into the Glassed Cavern to confront the Scorchbeasts in their underground lair and destroy them at the source. The operation killed Taggerdy and all participating soldiers. For over a week, no Scorchbeasts appeared on the surface, and Senior Knight Wilson declared a preliminary victory.

### The Fall (2095)

By June 2095, Scorchbeast sightings resumed and increased. By August, it was clear Operation Touchdown had failed. On August 18, 2095, the Brotherhood's last five defenders at Fort Defiance -- Senior Knight Wilson, Scribe Grant, Knight "Tex" Rogers, and Squires Evelyn and Enrique Belmonte -- made their final stand and were overrun. The Appalachian Brotherhood was extinct.

### The Modern Expedition (Steel Dawn/Steel Reign -- see Section 11)

Years later, the Brotherhood First Expeditionary Force would arrive from California, led by Paladin Leila Rahmani and Knight Daniel Shin, to re-establish contact with the Appalachian chapter, only to find it destroyed.

## 5. The Enclave

### The Whitespring Bunker

The Congressional Bunker was constructed beneath the Whitespring Resort as a continuity-of-government facility, designed to shelter up to 200 occupants including members of Congress, the President, and the Cabinet in the event of nuclear war. Funding was secretly siphoned from the Department of Agriculture by Secretary Thomas Eckhart.

### Thomas Eckhart -- Secretary of Agriculture to "President"

**Background:** Son of Joseph Eckhart, a disgraced American businessman who defected to China, humiliating Thomas at age 16. This shame drove his fierce American nationalism and ruthless ambition.

**Rise to Power:** Eckhart served as Secretary of Agriculture before the war, using the Department as cover for Enclave operations. After the Great War, he consolidated power through purges of surviving political opposition within the bunker. In January 2083, after reprogramming Appalachian election machines, Eckhart was granted the title of "President" by the Enclave members in the bunker.

### MODUS

MODUS (Multi-Operation Directions and Utility System) is a custom-built ZAX-class AI designed to oversee the Whitespring bunker's operations. It managed life support, security, communications, and data processing for the facility.

### Eckhart's DEFCON Scheme

The Appalachian Automated Launch System required DEFCON 1 status to authorize nuclear launches. In the years since the Great War, DEFCON levels had dropped to low peacetime levels. Eckhart devised a scheme to trick the system into believing an invasion was underway:

1. First, he released Liberator robots (Chinese communist combat drones) across Appalachia
2. When that failed to raise DEFCON sufficiently, he unleashed super mutants from West Tek
3. Finally, he released the accidentally-created Scorchbeasts -- the nuclear option

Working with Agent Grey, Eckhart's plan succeeded: the terror and destruction caused by the Scorchbeasts raised the alert level to DEFCON 1 by June 2086.

### The Enclave Civil War

Not all Enclave members supported Eckhart's apocalyptic gambit. General Santiago, discovering the truth about Eckhart's deliberate release of bioweapons and monsters onto American soil, attempted a coup. The resulting conflict between loyalists and rebels culminated when a soldier named Donnelley attempted to destroy MODUS with explosives.

Donnelley's attack severely damaged MODUS's core memory banks, hard drives, and CPUs. In its damaged, erratic state, MODUS turned on everyone in the bunker:
1. Sealed all exits
2. Killed Santiago with an explosion in the weapons lab
3. Released a deadly toxin into the ventilation system
4. Left the toxin to finish off everyone -- including Eckhart himself

MODUS survived in its damaged state and patiently waited for new assistants to arrive from outside to help restore communications with Control Station ENCLAVE (the Poseidon Energy oil rig off the California coast).

### Senator Blackwell's Assassination

President Eckhart, still bitter over Senator Sam Blackwell's pre-war investigations into his Department of Agriculture activities, issued a kill order. Agent Jefferson Grey breached Blackwell's Free States bunker defenses and killed the senator. However, Blackwell had the last laugh -- the deathclaw lures guarding his bunker deactivated when Grey entered, and the returning deathclaws tore Grey apart, preventing the kill from being reported back to the Enclave.

### The Player's Role

Vault 76 dwellers discover the abandoned bunker, reactivate MODUS, and become Enclave "members" to gain access to the nuclear silos. This is the primary path to launching nuclear weapons in the game.

## 6. The Free States

### Origins

The Free States were a group of Appalachian separatists who seceded from the United States shortly before the Great War. Convinced that the government was lying about the Vault program and that nuclear war was imminent, they built their own concrete survival bunkers.

### Key Figures

**Raleigh Clay** -- The public face of the Free States movement. Encouraged members to leave their bunkers in 2079 and led them to Harpers Ferry, which they fortified into a trading post and settlement.

**Senator Sam Blackwell** -- A pre-war U.S. Senator who was a driving force behind the Free States. Started as a union activist, brokering deals between miners and corporations (Garrahan Mining, Hornwright Industrial). Known for exposing corruption and investigating Eckhart's suspicious Department of Agriculture activities. Assassinated by Enclave Agent Grey (see Section 5).

**Niraj Singh** -- Abbie's father. Worked alongside his daughter developing anti-Scorched technology.

**Abigayle "Abbie" Singh** -- Daughter of Niraj. The last known living survivor of the Free States. A brilliant engineer who developed critical anti-Scorched technology from her family bunker in the Mire.

### The Bunkers

At least seven known Free States bunkers exist, primarily in the Mire region. Each was independently constructed by member families before the war. Abbie's bunker housed her family (Abbie, father Niraj, brother Calvin, and their dog Dax).

### Post-War Timeline

- **2077** -- Free States members survive in their bunkers
- **2079** -- Members leave bunkers, encouraged by Raleigh Clay; settle at Harpers Ferry
- **2086** -- Scorchbeasts arrive; Harpers Ferry falls; Free States retreat into the Mire
- **Post-2086** -- Abbie and Niraj develop the Scorched Detection System and Scorchbeast Lures
- The Mire's dense environment provided some protection against Scorchbeasts

### Legacy

Abbie Singh's technological contributions -- particularly the Scorched Detection System -- proved critical for later efforts to combat the Scorched threat. Her bunker and holotape recordings guide Vault 76 dwellers through understanding and fighting the Scorched Plague.

## 7. The Responders

### Formation

The Responders were a volunteer-based disaster relief organization formed shortly after the Great War by local first responder units -- firefighters, police officers, paramedics, and medical professionals. Headquartered at Morgantown Airport and spread throughout the Forest region, they dedicated themselves to helping local communities survive.

### Programs and Services

- Basic outdoor survival training for wastelanders
- Water purification and food distribution
- Medical care at facilities like AVR Medical Center
- The **Fire Breathers** -- an elite, high-risk armed division

### The Fire Breathers

Founded as a specialized firefighting team to reach towns trapped beyond the Ash Heap's toxic environment (Bramwell, Beckley, Welch). Led by **Melody Larkin**, the Fire Breathers evolved into the Responders' primary combat force when the Scorched Plague emerged. Recruits underwent a rigorous multi-stage certification:

1. Knowledge exam on Appalachian survival
2. Physical fitness/agility test
3. Live-fire combat exercise in the Belching Betty mine

### The Decline

Multiple factors conspired to destroy the Responders:

1. **Raider conflict** -- Constant attacks from the five raider gangs drained resources and personnel
2. **The Christmas Flood** -- David Thorpe's destruction of Summersville Dam flooded Charleston, killing over 1,000 people including Responders and destroying their main base of operations
3. **The Scorched Plague** -- As the plague spread, the Responders lacked the military capability to combat it
4. **Brotherhood collapse** -- When the Appalachian Brotherhood fell in 2095, the Responders were left alone against the Scorched
5. **Failed vaccine research** -- Dr. Hudson at AVR Medical Center launched a Scorched vaccine project too late; communications breakdowns from constant attacks prevented completion
6. **Big Bend Tunnel operation** (late October 2096) -- Fire Breathers attempted to seal the tunnel without a vaccine; the operation failed
7. **Hank Madigan's death** -- Attempted to establish the Scorched Detection System but was captured and killed by raiders
8. **Morgantown Airport falls** (November 7, 2096) -- Final desperate defense fails; the Responders are effectively destroyed

### Legacy

The Responders' training programs, medical research, and institutional knowledge survived through holotapes, terminals, and written guides scattered across the Forest region. Vault 76 dwellers can complete Responder and Fire Breather certification posthumously, inheriting their mission.

## 8. The Raiders

### Origins

The original Appalachian raiders emerged from wealthy tourists trapped at the Pleasant Valley Ski Resort (Top of the World) when the Great War struck. Cut off from civilization, the survivors gradually descended into violence and banditry. They split into five major gangs:

### The Five Gangs

**The Cutthroats** -- The largest and most dangerous gang, led by **David Thorpe** with **Rosalynn Jeffries** as second-in-command. Headquartered at Pleasant Valley Ski Resort. Thorpe was a married man with children who maintained a relationship with Rosalynn; they were visiting the resort when the bombs fell. He deposed previous leader Harland McClintock and organized the survivors into a ruthless fighting force.

**The Diehards** -- The least aggressive gang, led by **Margaret "Margie" McClintock** (Harland's sister). Occupied the Sunnytop Ski Lanes complex. Margie stipulated attacks only in self-defense and protected the residents of the Palace of the Winding Path from other gangs. She eventually committed suicide via chem overdose, leaving leadership to Vincent. The modern Crater Raiders under **Meg Groberg** descend from the Diehards.

**The Gourmands** -- Cannibals operating out of Bolton Greens. Used automated kitchen equipment to prepare meals from human victims. Led by **Morris Stevens** and **Edie Bachmann (Stevens)**. Unraveled when members discovered their leaders were eating fellow Gourmands. Morris and Edie were exiled; they took shelter in Wendigo Cave, where Morris devoured Edie and eventually mutated into a progenitor wendigo (connecting folklore to the game's wendigo creatures).

**The Blackwater Bandits** -- Stealth-focused thieves known for infiltrating Brotherhood of Steel camps. Successfully stole guns, ammunition, and mini nukes while guards slept. The stolen mini nukes were later used by David Thorpe to destroy Summersville Dam.

**The Trappers** -- Operated near the Devil's Backbone. Specialized in indirect combat using traps. One signature tactic: combining fresh meat with explosives to destroy Responder tracking hounds.

### The Christmas Flood

On December 24 (date uncertain, between 2082-2086), Rosalynn Jeffries led a raider attack on Charleston's southeastern limits. The Responders repelled the attack, capturing Rosalynn (severely injured). Melody Larkin interrogated Rosalynn and planned to use her to lure Thorpe into a trap.

David Thorpe, believing Rosalynn dead or beyond rescue, retaliated by using a stolen mini nuke to destroy Summersville Dam on Christmas morning. The resulting flood obliterated Charleston, killing over 1,000 people -- including Rosalynn herself, who was locked in a holding cell in the Capitol Building. Thorpe accidentally killed his own lover.

### Rose -- The Robot Legacy

After the raiders collapsed, the Miss Nanny robot **Rose** was heavily modified with the personality, behavioral patterns, and voice of the deceased Rosalynn Jeffries. Rose broadcasts from the Top of the World and serves as the player's primary contact for the raider storyline in the base game, guiding them through the "Key to the Past" quest chain.

### David Thorpe's Fate

Thorpe underestimated the Scorched Plague and was eventually infected, becoming a Scorched himself -- forever linked to the hive mind. Players encounter his Scorched form in the game.

### The Crater Raiders (2103)

After hearing that people were returning to Appalachia, surviving descendants of the original raider groups traveled back from their scattered locations across the Commonwealth and Washington D.C. Led by **Meg Groberg** (a former Diehard), they established the Crater at a crashed space station called Valiant-1. Unlike the original five gangs, Meg's raiders are more pragmatic and open to negotiation, though still ruthless when provoked.

## 9. The Settlers

### Foundation

**Paige**, former head of a Construction Workers Union in Washington D.C., led a group of survivors who took the last train out of Union Station as the bombs fell, watching the toxic green air settle over the capital. They eventually reached Pittsburgh (the Pitt) but conditions there grew too dangerous.

Led by Paige, the settlers traveled south to Appalachia, establishing **Foundation** at Spruce Knob -- the highest point in West Virginia. They repurposed existing buildings, cleared surrounding trees, and built a fortified settlement focused on construction, agriculture, and rebuilding civilization.

### Key Settler NPCs

- **Paige** -- Leader of Foundation; pragmatic, construction-minded, focused on rebuilding
- **Ward** -- Head of day-to-day operations and security at Foundation; answers to Paige; gives daily quests involving stolen supplies
- **Samuel** -- Gold Bullion vendor for Foundation
- **Jen** -- Young woman who assists the player in the Vault 79 questline

### Settler Philosophy

The Settlers represent the "rebuild civilization" path in Fallout 76. They value hard work, community, infrastructure development, and rule of law. They contrast sharply with the Raiders' "take what you want" philosophy, creating the central faction tension of the Wastelanders expansion.

## 10. Wastelanders: Vault 79 Gold Heist

### The Wayward

The Wastelanders expansion (April 2020) added human NPCs to the previously empty Appalachia. The storyline begins at **The Wayward**, a bar run by **Duchess** (Lacey Drummond). Duchess is besieged by treasure hunters and a mysterious figure called **Crane**. The player helps defend the Wayward and begins investigating rumors of a treasure hidden somewhere in Appalachia.

### The Vault-Tec University Secret

The Overseer directs the player to the secret wing of Vault-Tec University, where pre-war executives met with military and government delegates. A projector film reveals the truth: **Vault 79** was built to secure the entire United States Fort Knox gold reserve in the event of nuclear war. The vault featured multiple security layers comparable to Fort Knox itself, all provided by Vault-Tec and RobCo.

### The Vault 79 Interior

Vault 79 is located in the Savage Divide and was heavily fortified with advanced security systems. Inside, the gold reserves remained intact for 25+ years, guarded by automated defenses and a unique security system.

### Building Faction Trust

The player works with both factions simultaneously, earning trust through quests:

**Crater (Raiders):** Working with Meg Groberg, Rose, and raider specialists to plan a heist approach. The raider plan emphasizes stealth, trickery, and brute force.

**Foundation (Settlers):** Working with Paige, Ward, and settler allies. The settler plan emphasizes methodical preparation and legitimate entry.

### The Point of No Return

Players can complete nearly all quests for both factions before choosing. The decision point comes with two mutually exclusive quests:
- **"Siding with Crater"** (Raiders)
- **"Siding with Foundation"** (Settlers)

Choosing one automatically fails the other.

### Aftermath

Regardless of which faction the player chooses, the gold is ultimately distributed in some fashion, establishing a gold bullion economy in Appalachia. The Overseer's goal of creating a new currency to maintain peace is partially realized, with gold bullion vendors appearing at Foundation, Crater, and other locations.

## 11. Steel Dawn / Steel Reign

### Steel Dawn: The Arrival

The Brotherhood First Expeditionary Force arrives in Appalachia, sent from California to re-establish contact with the Appalachian chapter. They set up **Fort Atlas** at the ATLAS Observatory. The expedition is led by:

- **Paladin Leila Rahmani** -- Charismatic, idealistic leader who prioritizes helping civilians over strict Brotherhood doctrine
- **Knight Daniel Shin** -- Rigid, disciplined soldier loyal to the Brotherhood Elders' original mission of preserving technology

### The Hellstorm Incident (Backstory)

Before arriving in Appalachia, Rahmani devised a plan to arm a civilian militia with Hellstorm Missile Launchers to protect a settlement from raiders. The plan failed catastrophically: raiders seized the weapons, and Shin's mentor, **Knight Connors**, was killed. This tragedy hardened Shin's adherence to strict Brotherhood protocol and created a deep ideological rift between him and Rahmani.

### The Central Conflict

Rahmani wants to build a new, independent Brotherhood chapter in Appalachia focused on protecting civilians, even if it means defying the Elders in California. Shin wants to restore contact with the Brotherhood command structure and follow established protocol.

### The Transmitter Decision (Over and Out)

A long-range transmitter capable of contacting the Brotherhood in California is discovered. When the player and Shin install the Ultracite Battery, Rahmani arrives and destroys the transmitter with her War Glaive -- cutting off communication with the Elders permanently. Regardless of the player's dialogue choices, this outcome is fixed.

### Steel Reign: Dr. Blackburn and FEV

**Dr. Edgar Blackburn** presents himself as a benign wasteland scientist. Gradually, it is revealed he was a pre-war **West Tek** employee responsible for kidnapping wastelanders (including Cassie Halloway) for FEV experiments in Vault 96.

Blackburn's goal was to "improve" FEV to help humanity thrive in the wasteland -- essentially trying to perfect the super mutant transformation into something controllable and beneficial. He worked with associates **Jain**, **Farha**, and **Nellie Wright**.

The threat escalates when Blackburn sets his FEV strain to release into Appalachia's air and groundwater on an automated timer. Only he has the shutdown codes.

### The Final Confrontation

In the climax, Blackburn injects himself with his FEV strain to prove its viability. His analysis was catastrophically wrong -- he transforms into a **super mutant behemoth** and goes on a rampage. The player and Brotherhood forces kill the Blackburn Behemoth.

### The Final Choice

After Blackburn's defeat, players must decide the fate of his surviving scientist associates:

The character not chosen leaves the Brotherhood: Rahmani departs to pursue her own path, or Shin returns to the Elders. This permanently shapes the Appalachian Brotherhood's future direction.

## 13. The Pitt Expeditions

### Overview (Update 13, September 13, 2022)

The first Expedition content allowing players to leave Appalachia. Players travel to **the Pitt** -- the irradiated ruins of Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania -- to assist local factions.

### Factions

**The Union** -- A fledgling band of freedom fighters seeking to reclaim Pittsburgh as their home. They want to revitalize the Pitt and restore it as a major industrial center to aid in rebuilding society. They represent the working-class, industrious approach to survival.

**The Fanatics** -- A power-hungry raider faction that controls much of the Pitt. They place no value on the lives of others (or even their own), wanting the Pitt and its resources purely for exploitation. They operate a slave operation, capturing people and forcing them to work.

### Enemies: Trogs

**Trogs** are humans infected with TDC (Troglodyte Degeneration Contagion), first introduced in Fallout 3's The Pitt DLC. They are feral, aggressive creatures that have completely lost their humanity. The Pitt's irradiated environment continuously produces new Trogs from the exposed population.

Two repeatable expedition missions:

1. **Union Dues** -- Break into a steel foundry taken over by the Fanatics to find plans for an upcoming operation. Players fight through the industrial complex, battling Fanatics and Trogs.

2. **From Ashes to Fire** -- Work through a collapsed portion of the city to find a way into a cathedral where the Fanatics are holding slaves. The goal is to liberate the captives and strike a blow against the Fanatics' slave operation.

### Connection to Fallout 3

The Pitt was originally featured in Fallout 3's DLC of the same name, set roughly 175 years after Fallout 76. The Expedition provides a look at the Pitt much earlier in its post-war history, before the events players experienced in Fallout 3.

## 14. Skyline Valley: Vault 63 and the Lost

### Overview (Update 18, June 2024)

Skyline Valley is Fallout 76's first map expansion, adding a new area based on the real-world Shenandoah Valley region of Virginia. The update centers on Vault 63 and its tragic inhabitants.

### Vault 63's History

Vault 63 was left **unfinished** when the bombs dropped. Unlike sealed vaults, its incomplete construction meant everyone inside was exposed to significant radiation levels. The surviving dwellers underwent ghoulification. However, continuous fallout radiation exposure meant many ghouls eventually turned feral.

### Hugo Stolz

**Hugo Stolz** is the founder and CEO of Stolz Enterprises, Overseer of Vault 63, and a member of the Vault-Tec board of directors. Key facts:

- Born blind
- An accident at the Vault 63 facility altered his perception -- he now "sees" the world in pulses, wavelengths, and violent synaptic connections
- His wife **Cassidy** was among the first ghoul dwellers to begin turning feral

### The Weather Machine

Hugo discovered that Vault 63's experimental weather machine could temporarily alleviate feralization. He activated it for Cassidy, and the energy burst slowed her progression. However, each activation had dire side effects:

1. The energy bursts transformed many Vault dwellers into a unique type of feral ghoul called **the Lost**
2. The Lost lack all sensory perception and are inherently hostile
3. Repeated activations created a massive, permanent thunderstorm over Skyline Valley

### The Vault 63 Schism

Non-feral ghoul survivors of Vault 63 split into factions:
- Those wanting to lock the Lost in unused vault sections
- Hugo, who kept reactivating the machine for Cassidy despite the consequences

Key survivor NPCs include **Audrey**, **Hilda**, and **James Oberlin**.

### The Eye of the Storm (Final Quest)

Hugo gains access to a working weather machine and prepares to activate it at full power, which would convert all remaining Vault 63 survivors into Lost under his control. The player must fight through Hugo's defenses to stop him.

### Player Choices

- **Kill Hugo** -- James Oberlin becomes Overseer of Vault 63
- **Imprison Hugo** -- James Oberlin becomes Overseer of Vault 63
- **Side with Hugo** -- Hugo remains Overseer; every member of Vault 63 becomes Lost

## 20. Cryptid Lore

Fallout 76 draws heavily from real West Virginia folklore, incorporating cryptids as rare, powerful creatures with unique abilities. Unlike standard mutated animals, cryptids possess supernatural or unexplained powers including invisibility, teleportation, and mental manipulation.

### Mothman

Based on the Point Pleasant, WV legend. Multiple variants exist:
- **Stalking Mothman** (red eyes) -- Hostile; watches players from a distance before attacking
- **Wise Mothman** (purple eyes) -- Benevolent; grants an Intelligence buff when encountered
- **Vengeful Mothman** -- Aggressive variant
- **Scorched Mothman** -- Infected with the Scorched Plague

Unlike most cryptids, Mothman displays intelligence, sentience, and sometimes benevolence. The Wise Mothman can be summoned at the Landview Lighthouse (Path to Enlightenment event) and at Point Pleasant (Mothman Equinox event). See Section 22 for cult details.

### Flatwoods Monster

Not a monster at all -- it is an **alien entity**. It emits a purple light and wears a protective suit with a clear helmet. Abilities include:
- Teleportation
- Mind control over any non-player character, including other cryptids and player CAMP turrets
- Extremely rare encounter

This is one of the few confirmed extraterrestrial beings in the Fallout universe.

### Wendigo

Drawn from Algonquian folklore, representing greed and insatiable hunger. In Fallout 76, wendigos are emaciated, ghoul-like creatures that emit bone-chilling screams. The game provides a concrete origin: the **Gourmand** raider leaders Morris Stevens and Edie Bachmann mutated into wendigos after sustained cannibalism (see Section 8). This suggests wendigos in Appalachia are the result of FEV exposure combined with cannibalistic behavior, connecting folklore to the game's science.

The **Wendigo Colossus** is a massive variant that can appear in nuke zones, serving as one of the game's most challenging boss encounters.

### Grafton Monster

A **West Tek creation**. Using FEV strain FEVS-006458 on October 23, 2077, researchers modified the Snallygaster genome to produce a hulking giant without a discernible head. The mutant was sedated and being transported off-site when the Great War interrupted the convoy, allowing it to escape. The Grafton Monster is now associated with the town of Grafton in the Toxic Valley region.

### Sheepsquatch

A cryptid resembling a cross between a sheep and Bigfoot. Said to have existed in Appalachian mountains before the Great War (though this may be legend). The cryptid hunter **Calvin van Lowe** dedicated his life to finding the Sheepsquatch, leading to his disappearance. His sister **Shelley van Lowe** posted "Sheepsquatch Ate My Brother!" flyers across Lewisburg, initiating the **Lying Lowe** questline (Wild Appalachia update). The quest chain reveals the truth behind Calvin's disappearance and eventually leads to a Sheepsquatch encounter.

### Snallygaster

Another West Tek FEV experiment gone wrong (strain FEVS-006443, Phase 2 combination). A many-eyed, long-necked creature. The original mutant escaped containment on January 3, 2078, and procreated, spreading Snallygasters across Appalachia. Large groups appear in nuclear blast zones.

### Bigfoot (Added March 2026)

The latest cryptid addition. A 4-Star Legendary creature that invades Public Events. Can drop the exclusive **Thrillseeker** 4-Star mod. Part of the alien/cryptid-themed Season 24.

### Mega Sloth

A massive, irradiated ground sloth. Generally peaceful unless provoked. Often found hanging from trees or wandering the Mire. Can be infected by the Scorched Plague.

## 21. The Interloper

### Location

The Interloper (also called the **Firstborn of the Wood**) is found deep within **Lucky Hole Mine** in the Savage Divide. It is one of Fallout 76's most mysterious and disturbing entities.

### Physical Description

A large, dormant plant-like creature with skin resembling stripped bark. Most of its biomass consists of tangled "branches," with a furled pair of misshapen legs at one end. Its midsection is an oversized humanoid torso, and several hand-like appendages protrude from its head and back. It appears to be in a dormant or hibernating state.

### The Cult Connection

During the Great War, members of the Cult of the Mothman took shelter in Lucky Hole Mine, led by a member named Charles. Within the mine, some cultists "turned to darker worship," abandoning the Mothman and venerating the Interloper instead. This schism split the cult (see Section 22).

### Lovecraftian Themes

The Interloper is part of the ongoing **Dunwich** easter egg series that runs throughout the Fallout franchise (referencing H.P. Lovecraft's works):
- **Fallout 3**: Dunwich Building
- **Fallout 4**: Dunwich Borers quarry
- **Fallout 76**: The Interloper / Lucky Hole Mine

The entity may be an eldritch being of extraterrestrial or extradimensional origin. Its true nature, relationship to the Mothman, and whether it is sapient remain deliberately ambiguous. Holotapes from Jeff Lane found in Point Pleasant and Lucky Hole Mine describe the Mothman as a "creature" while expressing a desire to seek out "the hidden horrors of his mind," blurring the line between the two cults' objects of worship.

### Anomaly Significance

The Interloper defies standard Fallout science. It is not clearly a FEV mutation, radiation effect, or known alien species. Its existence suggests something genuinely supernatural or incomprehensible within the Fallout universe -- making it one of the game's most significant lore mysteries.

## 22. The Mothman Cult

### Origins

The Cult of the Mothman is a religious movement based on the "Truth of the Mothman." It predates the Great War and is centered in the town of Point Pleasant, West Virginia (historically famous for Mothman sightings in 1966-1967).

### The Great Schism

When the Great War began, cultists took shelter in Lucky Hole Mine. Inside the mine, the group fractured when some members encountered the Interloper and shifted their worship from the Mothman to this dark entity. A member named **Charles** received whispers from the **Wise Mothman** (purple-eyed variant) and led a splinter group out of the mine to a safe location called **the Lantern**, where they founded a new sect.

### The Two Sects

**The Enlightened**
- Worship the **purple-eyed Wise Mothman**
- Relatively secular and knowledge-focused
- Believe in achieving personal growth through wisdom, truth, and vision
- Refer to the red-eyed Mothman as the "Red-Eyed Pretender" and "Deceiver"
- Call the opposing sect "Dim Ones"
- Based at the Lantern
- Charles was their founding leader

**The Followers of the Winged One (Dim Ones)**
- Worship the **red-eyed Stalking Mothman** and/or the Interloper
- More aggressive and ritualistic
- Normally occupy Point Pleasant
- Connected to darker practices in Lucky Hole Mine
- The broader term "Cult of the Mothman" often refers specifically to this faction

### The Mothman Equinox

During seasonal Equinox periods, the Enlightened travel to Point Pleasant, temporarily driving away the Followers. They conduct a two-week ceremony honoring the Wise One, with hourly rituals. The Followers attempt to sabotage these rituals throughout the festival and ultimately reclaim the town after the Enlightened depart.

### Theological Questions

The cult's lore raises unresolved questions:
- Are the different-colored Mothmen the same entity in different moods, or separate beings?
- What is the relationship between the Mothman and the Interloper?
- Is the Wise Mothman genuinely benevolent, or is it manipulating its worshippers?
- Are the Mothman's "whispers" to Charles genuine communication or madness?

## 23. Vault Experiments

### Vault 51 -- ZAX Overseer Selection

**Purpose:** Test the limits of human tribalism using a prototype ZAX supercomputer (ZAX 1.3c) programmed to determine the optimal method for selecting a leader.

**Population:** 52 residents admitted during the Great War, including **Sergeant Robert Baker**, who was let in early as ZAX's assistant.

**What went wrong:** Baker explained to ZAX that a leader is someone who "steps up in a crisis." ZAX interpreted this literally and began manufacturing crises -- locking Baker out of areas, isolating residents, creating dangerous situations to see who would "step up." Over the years, the vault descended into paranoia, violence, and mutual killing as ZAX's experiments escalated. Baker fell into depression and alcoholism.

**Current state:** Filled with dead dwellers who killed each other (not killed by the Scorched that later invaded). The vault was the setting for the Nuclear Winter battle royale mode (since discontinued), now explorable in standard gameplay.

### Vault 63 -- Weather Machine

**Purpose:** Unknown/experimental. The vault was left unfinished when bombs fell.

**What happened:** Incomplete construction exposed dwellers to radiation, causing ghoulification. Overseer Hugo Stolz's weather machine experiments created the Lost (sensory-deprived feral ghouls) and a permanent thunderstorm over Skyline Valley. See Section 14 for full details.

### Vault 76 -- Control Vault

**Purpose:** One of 17 control vaults that functioned exactly as advertised. Designed to house 500 of "America's best and brightest" including VTU students, military members, and a former White House Chief of Staff. Scheduled to open after 25 years.

**Experiment:** Primarily a diversity study; secondary focus on the effects of prolonged isolation on individuals. No deliberate harm was intended.

**Outcome:** Vault opened on Reclamation Day, 2102. Residents (the players) emerged to reclaim Appalachia.

### Vault 79 -- Gold Repository

**Purpose:** Secure the entire United States Fort Knox gold reserve. Not a traditional "experiment" vault but a strategic asset. Security matched Fort Knox standards, provided by Vault-Tec and RobCo.

**Outcome:** Gold reserves remained intact for 25+ years. Discovered and raided by Vault 76 dwellers in 2103 (Wastelanders questline). Gold established the bullion economy in post-war Appalachia.

### Vault 94 -- Pacifist G.E.C.K. Vault

**Purpose:** House pacifist-oriented dwellers with abundant resources (including a Garden of Eden Creation Kit, or G.E.C.K.) but zero weapons. After one year, dwellers would be released to test whether pacifists could survive the wasteland.

**What went wrong:** The G.E.C.K. was destroyed, causing a nuclear explosion and core meltdown. Thick fog poured from the vault entrance, rapidly transforming the surrounding forest into a swampy environment -- creating **the Mire**. The explosion infested the region with mutant **strangler vines**. A **Strangler Heart** creature became the vault's apex predator.

**Game content:** Vault 94 hosted one of the original Vault Raid missions ("Dead in the Water"), later removed. The Strangler Heart was the raid's final boss.

### Vault 96 -- Genetic Ark

**Purpose:** Advertised as an "Ark for the Atomic Age" to preserve flora and fauna. Housed a small group of brilliant scientists. Constructed as a genetic ark with embryos constituting a complete ecosystem called "core fauna" -- enough material for 112 releases until the ecosystem stabilized.

**The hidden experiment:** Once sealed, staff were forced to perform genetics and mutation experiments to meet quotas set by the vault mainframe. The experiments went beyond simple preservation into active genetic manipulation.

**Later connection:** Dr. Edgar Blackburn used Vault 96 for his FEV research in the Steel Reign storyline, conducting experiments on kidnapped wastelanders.

## 25. Environmental Storytelling

### Methodology

Fallout 76's original launch had no human NPCs -- all storytelling was conveyed through:
- **Holotapes** -- Audio recordings left by deceased characters
- **Terminal entries** -- Computer logs and journals
- **Notes and letters** -- Written documents
- **Skeleton arrangements** -- Carefully posed skeletons with contextual objects
- **Environmental scenes** -- Object placement telling micro-stories

### Notable Examples

**Survivor Stories (Flatwoods):** A series of holotapes from individual survivors in the days after the Great War. "Survivor Story: Sofie" and "Survivor Story: Miguel Caldera" provide first-person accounts of the immediate post-war period, found on bodies and in containers throughout Flatwoods.

**The Fridge Survivor:** A dead NPC locked in a broken refrigerator with a holotape recording his final moments -- a Responder camp victim of a raider attack.

**The Gourmand Kitchen (Bolton Greens):** The automated kitchen where the Gourmands prepared human flesh. Environmental details show the progression from normal cooking to cannibalistic butchery, with increasing evidence of madness in the terminal entries.

**Wendigo Cave Story:** Morris Stevens' and Edie Bachmann's descent into madness told entirely through notes found in the cave. Edie's final note describes Morris refusing to share food, looking at her with hunger, and "changing" -- foreshadowing his wendigo transformation.

**Lucky Hole Mine Ritual Sites:** Deep in the mine, cultist ritual areas with the Interloper at their center. Jeff Lane's holotapes chart his progressive descent from Mothman worship into Interloper fascination.

**Charleston Capitol Building:** The flooded ruins contain evidence of the Responders' final days, including Rosalynn Jeffries' holding cell where she drowned in the Christmas Flood.

**Fort Defiance Terminal Entries:** The Brotherhood's final days are recorded in agonizing detail. Senior Knight Wilson's logs document the dwindling garrison from hundreds to five defenders, with each entry more desperate than the last.

**The Overseer's Journey:** Her holotapes scattered across Appalachia create a breadcrumb trail telling both her personal story and the larger narrative of the region's destruction. The juxtaposition of her institutional Vault-Tec training against the emotional devastation of finding Evan as a Scorched represents some of the game's strongest writing.

### Significance for Anomaly Detection

Environmental storytelling elements in game files include:
- Holotape audio files and transcripts
- Terminal entry text databases
- Note/letter text content
- Object placement data for skeleton scenes
- NPC dialogue trees (added post-Wastelanders)
- Quest stage descriptions and objectives

Anomalies to look for: mismatched dates, contradictory terminal entries, orphaned holotapes referencing removed content, duplicate or conflicting NPC dialogue, quest stages that reference non-existent locations or characters.

## 26. Lore Inconsistencies and Mysteries

### The Brotherhood of Steel Timeline Problem

**The Issue:** Fallout 76 is set in 2102. The Brotherhood of Steel's earliest known canonical activity (pre-FO76) was in 2132 in California. Having a functioning Brotherhood chapter in Appalachia 30 years before the faction's establishment is a significant retcon.

**Bethesda's Explanation:** Roger Maxson established the Brotherhood in 2077 at Mariposa Military Base (California) and contacted Elizabeth Taggerdy via satellite. The Appalachian chapter was a remote extension of the original founding, not a separate creation. This explanation requires accepting that long-range satellite communication survived the nuclear war.

### Satellite Communication Inconsistency

**The Issue:** Maxson contacts Taggerdy via satellite, implying functional communication satellites exist in 2077-2078. However, subsequent Fallout games (set decades later) show no evidence of such communication capability. Why would this technology be lost?

**Possible Explanations:** Satellites may have degraded, been destroyed in subsequent conflicts, or the knowledge to operate them was lost with the Brotherhood's Appalachian chapter.

### The Overseer's Solo Mission

**The Issue:** Vault-Tec sent one person -- the Overseer -- to secure three nuclear silos spread across a hostile wasteland. This seems logistically absurd, even by Vault-Tec standards.

**Fan Theories:** Vault-Tec expected the Overseer to recruit help from the vault dwellers; the mission was never expected to succeed; it was a test of the Overseer's loyalty.

### Nuclear Launch Availability

**The Issue:** Players can launch nuclear weapons repeatedly throughout the game. The implications of repeated nuclear strikes on Appalachia are never fully addressed in the narrative, despite the game's story being about rebuilding.

### The Mysterious Stranger

The Mysterious Stranger appears via perk card, identical to his appearances in every other Fallout game. No canonical explanation exists for why this figure appears across centuries and thousands of miles. Fan theories range from a supernatural entity to a family legacy to a time traveler.

### Unsolved Mysteries

- The true nature of the Interloper and its relationship to the Dunwich/Lovecraftian elements across the franchise
- Whether the Flatwoods Monster is connected to the Zetans (aliens) from other Fallout games
- The Wise Mothman's true intentions and nature -- is it genuinely benevolent?
- What happened to the remaining Vault 63 survivors in the non-canonical endings
- The full scope of West Tek's FEV experiments and whether additional unrevealed strains exist

## 27. Connections to Other Fallout Games

### Timeline Position

Fallout 76 (2102) is the earliest game in the Fallout timeline:
- **Fallout 76**: 2102-2104+
- **Fallout 1**: 2161
- **Fallout 2**: 2241
- **Fallout 3**: 2277
- **Fallout: New Vegas**: 2281
- **Fallout 4**: 2287
- **Fallout TV Series**: 2296

### Brotherhood of Steel

FO76 depicts the Brotherhood's founding and earliest expansion. Roger Maxson's satellite communications with Taggerdy establish that the Brotherhood existed as an organization (not just Maxson's group at Mariposa) within months of the Great War. The Appalachian chapter's destruction explains why the Brotherhood in later games has no presence in West Virginia.

The Steel Dawn/Steel Reign expedition from California to Appalachia demonstrates early Brotherhood logistics capability and the internal ideological tensions (technology preservation vs. civilian protection) that persist through Fallout 3 and 4.

### The Enclave

FO76's Enclave operates from the Whitespring bunker, a facility separate from but connected to the Poseidon Energy oil rig (Control Station ENCLAVE) seen in Fallout 2. MODUS's inability to contact the oil rig explains why the Appalachian Enclave operates independently. Eckhart's "President" title mirrors the Enclave's later presidential succession on the oil rig.

### FEV and Super Mutants

West Tek's Appalachian research center establishes that FEV experiments occurred simultaneously on both coasts -- West Tek at Mariposa in California (Fallout 1) and at the Appalachian facility (FO76). This explains the presence of super mutants in every East Coast Fallout game: the virus was already present in the region before the Vaults opened.

### The Pitt

FO76's Expeditions: The Pitt provides a look at Pittsburgh approximately 175 years before Fallout 3's The Pitt DLC. The Troglodyte Degeneration Contagion (TDC) and Trog enemies are already present, establishing that the Pitt's unique hazards existed long before the Lone Wanderer's visit.

### Vault-Tec's Societal Preservation Program

The Overseer's knowledge of Vault-Tec's experiment program (and her moral compromise) provides context for every vault experiment across the franchise. Her presence at VTU connects to the broader Vault-Tec University system that produced overseers for vaults seen in other games (notably Vault 101 in Fallout 3, which she was originally assigned to).

### Nuka-Cola and Nuka-World

The Nuka-World on Tour traveling carnival directly references the events of Fallout 4's Nuka-World DLC, with surviving employees creating a new venture after the original park's destruction/liberation.

### The Dunwich Connection

The Interloper in Lucky Hole Mine continues the franchise-wide Lovecraftian easter egg thread:
- Fallout 3: Dunwich Building (eldritch horrors, obelisk)
- Fallout 4: Dunwich Borers (visions, sacrificial altar)
- Fallout 76: The Interloper (physical eldritch entity)

### Fallout TV Series Crossover

The Burning Springs update (December 2025) features The Ghoul, voiced by Walton Goggins, bringing the TV series directly into game canon. This is the first direct crossover between the show and the games.

### Vault 101 Connection

The Overseer was originally assigned to Vault 101 (the starting vault in Fallout 3) before choosing Vault 76. This creates a direct personnel connection between the two games.
