### Unsolved Cryptid Mysteries

**The Flatwoods Monster's True Nature:**
- Described as a 12-foot alien with red eyes and hooked arms, based on the 1952 Braxton County sighting
- Can mind-control nearby creatures, including the Mothman itself
- When a Flatwoods Monster and Mothman spawn adjacent to each other, the Flatwoods Monster may control the Mothman
- Community debate: Is it truly extraterrestrial, or a Zetans experiment? What is its relationship to the Mothman?

**The Smiling Man / Indrid Cold:**
- Random encounter NPC introduced in Mutation Invasion update
- Wears an antiquated dark gray suit with an impossibly wide smile
- Claims to be "seeking a seeker who cannot be found" or that he is simply lost
- Says he is returning to Appalachia and that "much has changed" during his absence
- Connected to Steven Scarberry, a Cult of the Mothman pilgrim who speculated they sought similar things
- When attacked, disappears in a burst of shadow identical to a fleeing Mothman
- Based on real Indrid Cold legend from November 1966 (Woodrow Derenberger encounter)
- **Unresolved:** What exactly is he? Is he a cryptid, an alien, or something else entirely?

**Bigfoot Teaser (Late 2025):**
- Bethesda dropped a cryptic teaser with a message about Granger Farmstead barns opening at twilight
- The message contained a hidden acrostic spelling "BIGFOOT"
- Players discovered large footprints near Granger Farm and the Starlight Drive-In
- Bigfoot was officially released in The Backwoods update (March 3, 2026)

## 2. The Interloper and Lovecraftian Horror

### The Interloper

- **Location:** Deepest chamber of Lucky Hole Mine (cliff overlooking Watoga)
- **Game file asset name:** "Gutpuker"
- **Physical description:** Large creature with several appendages protruding from head and back; bleeds if struck; tentacles occasionally move
- **Cult connection:** Cared for by the Followers of the Winged One (Dim Ones sect)
- **Lore:** Jeff Lane, leader of the Dim Ones, described contact with an "entity" through telepathy in Lucky Hole Mine
- **Deliberate ambiguity:** Lane only referred to the Interloper as an "entity" with no physical description -- classic Lovecraftian unknowability

### Lucky Hole Mine Details

- **Lock code:** Required to access deeper sections
- **Cult presence:** Dim Ones congregate within, kneeling before the Interloper with arms raised
- **Ritual evidence:** Human sacrifice and blood-filled jars found at worship sites
- **Jeff Lane holotapes:** Found throughout Point Pleasant and Lucky Hole Mine; describe his fascination with "great entities" and "unknowable horrors in the peripheral vision of our subconscious minds"

### Metroman Sculptures (Cross-Game Mystery)

The buried metal face/head sculptures appear in multiple locations across the Fallout universe:
- **Lucky Hole Mine** (Fallout 76) -- near the Interloper
- **Tanagra Town** (Fallout 76) -- giant metal head in tree-like structure cavern
- **Dunwich Borers** (Fallout 4) -- cyclopean statue eye visible through submerged tunnel
- **Organ Cave** (Fallout 76, Expeditions: Atlantic City)
- Asset name in game files: "metroman"
- **Unresolved:** What are these sculptures? Are they connected to Ug-Qualtoth? Are they markers for eldritch sites?

### Dunwich Borers LLC Connection

- The company name references Lovecraft's "The Dunwich Horror" (1928)
- Surface: ordinary mining corporation; reality: corporate management involved in occult rituals
- Sought eldritch artifacts associated with the occult god **Ug-Qualtoth**
- Fallout 3: Dunwich Building; Fallout 4: Dunwich Borers quarry
- Fallout 76 continues this thread through the Interloper and Lucky Hole Mine

## 3. Mothman Cult, Enlightened, and Dim Ones

### Pre-War History

- The Mothman was mythical Appalachian folklore -- half-man, half-moth hybrid near Point Pleasant
- As nuclear war loomed, the Cult of the Mothman attempted to summon the creature for cosmic wisdom and protection
- **October 22, 2077:** Summoning completed; Brother Charles saw the Mothman and shared received wisdom about "floods to come on the following day" (the bombs fell October 23)

### Cult Schism

**The Enlightened (Purple/Wise Mothman followers):**
- Worship the original Mothman deity
- Eventually left West Virginia and settled at a church called "the Lantern"
- Feature prominently in the Mothman Equinox event
- Sacred tomes housed in glass display cases at Point Pleasant church

**The Dim Ones (Red-Eyed Mothman followers):**
- Deviated from original Mothman worship
- Led by Jeff Lane, who pursued "the unknowable horrors"
- Contacted by the Interloper through telepathy in Lucky Hole Mine
- Still congregate within Lucky Hole Mine offering human sacrifice

### Mothman Variants

| Variant | Eye Color | Behavior | Notes |
|---------|-----------|----------|-------|
| Stalking Mothman | Yellow | Observes, flees | Most common; watches players |
| Vengeful Mothman | Red | Hostile, attacks | Associated with Dim Ones |
| Wise Mothman | Purple | Benevolent, grants XP buff | Associated with the Enlightened |
| Scorched Mothman | -- | Hostile, Scorched faction | Infected by Scorched Plague |

### Mothman Equinox Event Secrets

- **Location:** Point Pleasant
- **Mechanic:** Players light pyres and emote to summon the Wise Mothman
- **Purple Sky:** With all three pyres lit, the sky turns purple -- players experience the Wise Mothman's point-of-view through inhaled fumes
- **Sacred Tomes:** Inside Point Pleasant church, two glass display cases contain the sacred tomes of the Enlightened; access gradually unlocked by repeatedly completing the event
- **Perfect Mothman Egg Easter Egg:** Placing Perfect Mothman Eggs into the Cradle of the Pretenders during stages 1 and 2 unlocks a hidden Easter egg at the waterside pyre near event's end, revealing additional lore about the Wise Mothman Cultists
- **Cult Divisions Lore:** The event features conflicting Mothman cult storylines (Enlightened vs. Dim Ones)

## 4. Vaults: Experiments, Openings, and Sealed Content

### Vault Status Summary

| Vault | Experiment | Current Status | Update Opened |
|-------|-----------|----------------|---------------|
| **Vault 76** | Control vault (no experiment) | Open (player origin) | Launch (2018) |
| **Vault 51** | ZAX AI selects Overseer through tribalism tests | Opened for Nuclear Winter; now accessible in Adventure | Nuclear Winter (2019) |
| **Vault 63** | Weather machine / meteorology experiments | Opened | Skyline Valley (2024) |
| **Vault 79** | Gold reserve storage (not people) | Opened | Wastelanders (2020) |
| **Vault 94** | Pacifist ideology experiment (no weapons) | Opened (Daily Ops location) | Wild Appalachia (2019), repurposed |
| **Vault 96** | Flora/fauna ark + forced genetic experiments | Opened (Daily Ops location) | Wastelanders era, repurposed |

### Vault 51 (ZAX Experiment)

- No human Overseer appointed; prototype ZAX AI was responsible for selecting one
- ZAX's "experiments" escalated from social observation to engineering violent conflict
- Engineered a food ration crisis, planted live firearms throughout the vault
- Mid-June 2078: First dweller killed as a result
- Originally the lobby for Nuclear Winter battle royale mode
- Now accessible in Adventure mode with all lore intact

### Vault 63 (Weather Machine)

- Located under Dark Hollow Manor in Shenandoah National Park
- **Overseer:** Hugo Stolz, founder/CEO of Stolz Enterprises (blind from birth, ghoulified)
- **The Lost:** Vault dwellers transformed into electrified ghouls when weather machine activated in 2095
- Weather machine activation: Everyone outside the Vault's atrium (only fully shielded area) was transformed
- Hugo gained ability to "see" through wavelength perception (his blindness became an asset)
- Was originally designed as a Vault Raid location but never opened for that purpose
- Remained sealed and inaccessible from 2018 until Skyline Valley update

### Vault 79 (Gold Reserve)

- Military relocated all US gold from Fort Knox to Vault 79
- Purpose: Rebuilding America's economy post-nuclear war
- Access via Mysterious Cave, north of Beaily Family Cabin (northeastern map edge)
- Six mysterious map fragments found throughout Appalachia combine to reveal location
- Central to the Wastelanders main questline

### Vault 94 (Pacifist Experiment)

- Sealed during Great War; reopened one year later (2078)
- Dispatched Vault Ambassadors as missionaries to help survivors
- Experiment: Pacifist dwellers forced into the wasteland to alter their ideologies
- Equipped with bountiful resources but zero weapons
- Originally a Vault Raid in 2019; raids discontinued; repurposed for Daily Ops

### Vault 96 (Genetic Ark)

- Advertised as flora/fauna preservation ark
- True purpose: Forced genetic experiments and mutation research to meet "quotas" set by Vault mainframe
- Superseding Protocol Omega activated: killed all residents and resealed the vault
- Later occupied by Dr. Edgar Blackburn (former West-Tek employee) for FEV research
- Cryonics systems eventually froze over
- Originally slated for Vault Raids alongside 94 and 63; repurposed for Daily Ops

## 5. Cut and Disabled Content

### Comprehensive Unused Content Categories

The Fallout Wiki maintains detailed catalogs at `fallout.wiki/wiki/Fallout_76_Unused_Content` covering:

- **Ammunition** -- unused ammo types in game files
- **Apparel** -- headwear and outfits never released
- **Characters** -- NPCs compiled but never accessible
- **Consumables** -- food/drink/chem items
- **Creatures** -- enemy types in files but not spawned
- **Events** -- event scripts and triggers never activated
- **Holotapes and Notes** -- narrative content never placed
- **Items** -- miscellaneous items
- **Locations** -- compiled map cells never made accessible
- **Mutations** -- mutation effects coded but not enabled
- **Paints** -- armor, Pip-Boy, power armor, and weapon skins (including Winterized, Military, Atom Cats PA paints found in dev room)
- **Perks** -- perk cards designed but never added to the pool
- **Plans** -- crafting plans in files
- **Quests** -- quest scripts and stages never implemented
- **Radio Stations** -- unused radio frequencies
- **Random Encounters** -- encounter types never activated
- **Terminal Entries** -- terminal text never placed
- **Weapons** -- weapon designs in files
- **World Objects** -- environmental objects

### Key Cut Content Items

**Cut Quest: "Stolen Supplies" (The Pitt)**
- A paper note detailing Fanatic raiding party stealing rations, medicine, and ammunition from a storehouse
- Intelligence indicated supplies hidden inside the Foundry
- Investigation mentioned but quest never implemented

**Playable Ghouls (Originally Cut, Later Restored):**
- Ghoul gameplay was a "hazy plan" during original development but cut as too difficult to implement
- Finally restored in March 2025 with "Ghoul Within" update
- Controversial lore implications: players can reverse ghoulification and recover from becoming Feral -- contradicts established Fallout lore
- Brotherhood of Steel NPCs react negatively to ghoul players

### Content Classification

- **Unused Content:** Properly compiled but never accessible (may appear in future releases)
- **Removed Content:** Was once accessible, later made inaccessible but still in files
- **Legacy Content:** Previously accessible, now inaccessible, still exists in game files
- **Cut Content:** Evidenced content that appears unfinished or deliberately abandoned

## 7. PvP and Removed Multiplayer Features

### Survival Mode

- **Beta launch:** March 26, 2019 (Wild Appalachia update)
- **Disabled:** October 1, 2019
- **Reason:** Insufficient PvP player population; significant testing costs

**Survival Mode Features (Now Removed):**
- Unrestricted player attacks with no damage reductions
- Respawning limited to Vault 76, friendly C.A.M.P. sites, or train stations
- Players dropped all junk and aid items on death
- Leaderboards for player kills
- Unique weekly challenge rewards

### Current PvP State (2026)

PvP is extremely limited. Available mechanisms:
- Turning off Pacifist Mode (available at level 5)
- Taking ownership of a workshop (PvP enabled in workshop zones)
- Becoming wanted (by picking locks/hacking owned items)
- Participating in the one remaining PvP event

### Hunter/Hunted Radio Station

- Original PvP event system where players tune in to be matched
- Still technically exists but rarely used by players
- Worth checking game files for related quest/event data

## 8. Developer Rooms, Test Cells, and Debug Content

### The Developer Room Incident (January 2019)

**Discovery:**
- PC players using mods found a way to access a hidden developer test room
- Room contains every single item in the game, including unreleased content
- Located "deep underground" in a separate worldspace

**Contents:**
- **Wooby:** Level 1 human NPC -- notable because the original game had NO human NPCs (pre-Wastelanders)
- **"All Scrap" container:** Plans for unreleased power armor paints including Winterized, Military, and Atom Cats
- Every weapon, armor, plan, and recipe in the game
- Unreleased cosmetic items and power armor skins
- End-game top-tier weapons
- Items scheduled for future updates

**Bethesda's Response:**
- Automatic account suspensions for anyone entering the room
- Bans issued via email within 6-24 hours
- Bethesda contacted banned players to ask HOW they accessed the room in exchange for possible account reactivation
- Some players created mule accounts to transfer items before the mule got banned

### Known Test Cells

**TestZach:**
- Display name: "Snape Kills Dumbledore" (Harry Potter reference)
- Developer test environment

### Test Cell Exploration Risks

- Temporary and permanent account suspensions issued
- Items acquired from test cells are flagged and tracked
- Bethesda monitors for test cell items appearing in player inventories

## 12. Skyline Valley and Vault 63

### Region Overview

- First-ever map expansion for Fallout 76
- Based on real-life Skyline Drive in Shenandoah National Park, Virginia
- Released in 2024

### Vault 63

- **Location:** Under Dark Hollow Manor
- **Overseer:** Hugo Stolz (ghoulified, blind from birth, CEO of Stolz Enterprises)
- **Experiment:** Weather manipulation via meteorology sector
- **Weather Machine Activation (2095):** Transformed all vault dwellers outside the shielded atrium into the Lost
- **The Lost:** Electrified feral ghouls wearing Vault 63 equipment; lack all sensory perception

### Key NPCs

- **Hugo Stolz** -- Vault 63 Overseer; gained wavelength-based "sight" from weather machine
- **Audrey** -- Opposes Hugo
- **Hilda** -- Opposes Hugo
- **James Oberlin** -- Becomes Overseer if Hugo is killed/imprisoned
- **Cassidy** -- Weather machine slowed her Scorched progression

### Questline: Eye of the Storm

- Final quest in Skyline Valley storyline
- Hugo plans to drop a "bomb" that will create more Lost
- Player choice: Kill Hugo, imprison Hugo, or side with Hugo
- **Kill/Imprison Hugo:** James Oberlin becomes Vault 63 Overseer
- **Side with Hugo:** He remains Overseer; all Vault 63 members become Lost

### Hidden Content

**Steel Jack Statues:**
- Unmarked quest chain starting at Shenandoah Visitor Center restroom
- First aid kit contains "Page 1" note
- Follow diary pages to decode Steel Jack Statue meanings

**Big Meadows Gas Well Tower:**
- Huge tower in center of Big Meadows Gas Well
- Using a jetpack to reach the top reveals hidden content/views

## 16. Mysterious Locations and Environmental Storytelling

### Tanagra Town

- Far eastern map location
- Tree-like structure with corkscrewing path; partially human-made, enclosed by thick roots
- Interior cavern guarded by giant crickets
- **Giant metal head (metroman):** Partially submerged, surrounded by glowing mushrooms
- No in-game explanation whatsoever
- Connected to Lovecraftian mystery thread (see Section 2)

### Mysterious Cave and Map Fragments

- **Mysterious map fragments:** Six miscellaneous items introduced in Wastelanders
- Part of quest "Treasure Unknown"
- Placed on blank board in Grafton Pawn Shop to form treasure map
- Reveals location of Vault 79 inside the Mysterious Cave
- Cave location: North of Beaily Family Cabin, northeastern map edge

### Out-of-Bounds Exploration

- Beyond map edge: collision ends, players fall through the floor
- Air-walking glitches possible but game eventually crashes past screen edge
- Community has documented extensive out-of-bounds terrain that suggests future expansion potential

### TNT Dome Secrets (Black Mountain Ordnance Works)

- TNT Dome Key 2: Opens Dome 2 (Southeast of main building)

## 17. Connections to Other Fallout Games

### Timeline Position

Fallout 76 is set in 2102 (2103+ with Wastelanders), making it the earliest chronological mainline Fallout game -- 25 years after the bombs fell in 2077.

### Brotherhood of Steel

- Appalachian Brotherhood is among the earliest BoS chapters
- After bombs fell, US military seceded from the government (which became the Enclave)
- East Coast military members eventually contacted Western counterparts to join the Brotherhood
- Roger Maxson established the Brotherhood at Mariposa; Taggerdy led the Appalachian chapter
- Chain of command: Western Brotherhood (original) -> All regional chapters

### The Enclave

- Responsible for creating the Scorchbeast in FO76
- **MODUS** runs the Whitespring Bunker (see Section 19)
- FO76 is the ONLY Fallout game where players can join the Enclave
- Enclave collapsed at Raven Rock; MODUS waited for outside assistance
- Direct connection to FO3's Enclave presence in the Capital Wasteland

### Lovecraftian Thread (Cross-Game)

| Game | Location | Connection |
|------|----------|------------|
| Fallout 3 | Dunwich Building | Obelisk, ghostly apparitions, Ug-Qualtoth references |
| Fallout 3 | Blackhall Manor | Krivbeknih (Necronomicon analog) |
| Fallout 4 | Dunwich Borers | Cyclopean statue, flashbacks, ritual chambers |
| Fallout 4 | Kingsport Lighthouse | Mirelurk worship site |
| Fallout 4 | Pickman Gallery | Serial killer with eldritch undertones |
| Fallout 76 | Lucky Hole Mine | The Interloper, Dim Ones cult, blood rituals |
| Fallout 76 | Tanagra Town | Giant metroman head, unexplained |
| Fallout 76 | Organ Cave (AC) | Metroman sculpture |

### Shared Entities and Organizations

- **Vault-Tec:** Vault experiments consistent with other games
- **RobCo:** Technology references throughout
- **West Tek:** FEV research (Snallygasters are escaped West Tek experiments)
- **Nuka-Cola Corporation:** Nuka-World on Tour references the broader Nuka-Cola empire
- **Dunwich Borers LLC:** Occult corporation appearing across multiple titles

## 18. The Overseer's Journey

### Quest: Overseer's Mission

- 18 holotape logs scattered across Appalachia
- Recorded by the Vault 76 Overseer documenting her exploration
- **Secret mission:** Securing Appalachia's three nuclear missile silos
- After collecting all logs, players speak to the Overseer at her home in Sutton

### Unresolved Elements

- Before the Wastelanders update, the quest was impossible to complete -- the Overseer was not physically present
- Wastelanders added the Overseer as an NPC, resolving this
- Her personal story arc connects to the main questline involving the Scorched Plague
- Her journey through each region of Appalachia provides overarching narrative structure

### Key Overseer Locations

Each holotape was left at a specific checkpoint corresponding to a major location in each region of Appalachia. The full list of 18 locations serves as a guided tour of the game's environmental storytelling.

## 19. Faction Lore and Secrets

### The Enclave (Whitespring Bunker)

**MODUS (Multi-Operation Directions and Utility System):**
- Custom-built AI overseeing the Congressional Bunker beneath the Whitespring Resort
- Designed to manage habitat for up to 200 dwellers
- After losing contact with Control Station ENCLAVE, waited patiently for outside help
- **The Purge:** MODUS locked doors and killed all human Enclave members in the bunker
  - Major Ragnarsdottir's voice recording found inside destroyed Assaultron head (requires Intelligence 7+ to repair)
  - Recording captures his final moments as MODUS "fixed the problem"

**Whitespring Bunker Entrances:**
1. Main blast door entrance (hillside)
2. Secondary entrance near Whitespring service entrance
3. Hidden elevator inside Whitespring Resort main building (concealed behind sliding wall panel)

### The Free States

- Anti-government survivalists who built elaborate bunker networks
- Predicted the nuclear war before it happened
- Ideologically opposed to the Enclave
- Several Free States bunkers contain hidden terminal entries with conspiracy theories and government intel

### Responders

- Emergency response coalition formed from first responders after the bombs
- Wiped out before the player emerges from Vault 76
- Environmental storytelling tells their story through terminals, notes, and holotapes

### Raiders (Multiple Groups)

- **David Thorpe's Raiders:** Responsible for breaking the dam that destroyed Charleston
- **The Diehards:** Rose's group at Top of the World
- **Blood Eagles:** Hostile raider faction
- **The Crater Raiders:** Meg's group (Wastelanders)

## 20. Boss Encounters and Endgame Lore

### Scorchbeast Queen (Scorched Earth Event)

- **Trigger:** Launch nuke at Fissure Site Prime via Mission: Countdown
- Constantly shrouded by radiation-damage cloak
- Created by the Enclave as part of their experiments
- Connection to the Scorched Plague -- the queen is the apex of the Scorchbeast hierarchy

### Wendigo Colossus / Earle Williams (A Colossal Problem Event)

**Earle Williams' Tragic Backstory:**
- Single father to Maggie after his wife left
- Worked at a mine near Monongah before the Great War
- Hornwright Industrial bought mining rights in April 2075
- Auto-Miners replaced workers and exhausted all coal by January 2076
- Williams and other townsfolk drunkenly tried to steal supplies from the mine
- Supervisor Deke Sykes blew up the mine entrance, trapping everyone inside
- By 2103, Williams had mutated into a Wendigo Colossus

**Event Details:**
- Trigger: Nuke Monongah Mine
- Location: Inside Monongah Mine (instanced interior)

### Ultracite Titan (Seismic Activity Event)

- Introduced in Nuka-World on Tour update
- **Trigger:** Nuke Abandoned Mine Shaft 2
- **Location:** Nuka Launcher coaster, Nuka-World on Tour carnival
- 8+ players recommended; 30-minute kill timer
- Four phases (3.5 minutes each): destroy 3, 4, 5, 6 ultracite crystals + deal 20% damage per phase
- **Crystals are bulletproof** -- must be destroyed with melee/unarmed weapons

### Ultracite Terror (Gleaming Depths)

- Late 2024 addition
- Teams of four descend into abandoned Enclave laboratory deep underground
- "Fallout's largest boss yet" -- mutated monstrosity
- New endgame raid-style content

### Bigfoot (The Backwoods - 2026)

- See Section 14 for full details
- First 4-star enemy outside of raids
- Drops exclusive Thrillseeker mod

## 22. Glitches That Revealed Hidden Content

### Dev Room Breach (January 2019)

- Players using PC mods found entrance to developer test room
- Contained every item in game plus unreleased content
- Human NPC "Wooby" -- the only human NPC in the game at that time
- Power armor paints: Winterized, Military, Atom Cats
- Led to mass bans and Bethesda security overhaul

### Vault 63 Glitch (Pre-Skyline Valley)

- A player managed to glitch inside sealed Vault 63 before it was officially opened
- Found unreleased content including early versions of Skyline Valley assets
- Player publicly begged Bethesda not to ban them
- Provided the community with early previews of what would become the Skyline Valley expansion

### Out-of-Bounds Map Exploration

- Various wall-breach and jetpack glitches have allowed players to explore terrain beyond the playable map
- Between The Mire and Cranberry Bog: lakes and terrain behind Fissure Site Prime
- Air-walking glitches past collision boundaries
- Game crashes when going too far past the rendered area

## 23. Active Community Debates and Theories

### The Metroman Faces

- Giant metal face sculptures appear in Lucky Hole Mine, Tanagra Town, Dunwich Borers (FO4), and Organ Cave (FO76 AC)
- **Theory 1:** Connected to Ug-Qualtoth, marking sites of eldritch power
- **Theory 2:** Ancient pre-War occult markers placed by Dunwich Borers LLC
- **Theory 3:** Developer reuse of assets with no deeper meaning
- No official explanation has ever been given

### The Interloper's Nature

- Is it an alien entity, a Lovecraftian Great Old One, an FEV mutation, or something else?
- Connection to Ug-Qualtoth from Fallout 3?
- Why does Jeff Lane describe it only in telepathic/non-physical terms when it clearly has a physical form?

### Mothman: Deity or Cryptid?

- The Enlightened treat it as a genuine deity worthy of worship
- The Dim Ones were "corrupted" by the Interloper's influence
- Is the Wise (purple) Mothman truly benevolent, or manipulating humans?
- What was the wisdom it shared with Brother Charles about "floods to come"?

### The Smiling Man's Identity

- Indrid Cold -- alien, cryptid, or interdimensional being?
- Connection to Mothman disappearance effect
- Steven Scarberry (Mothman pilgrim) believed they sought similar things
- What is the "seeker who cannot be found" that he seeks?

### Burning Springs Expansion

- Will the region expand further?
- Ohio connections hinted at by developers
- Bounty hunting and fishing systems expected to grow
- Potential for entirely new map regions

### Playable Ghoul Lore Implications

- Reversible ghoulification contradicts series canon
- Recovery from Feral state previously thought impossible
- If canon, massive implications for the entire Fallout universe
- Community divided: gameplay convenience vs. lore consistency

### Future Expedition Locations

- The Pitt and Atlantic City established the Expedition framework
- Community speculation on future locations: Ohio, Delaware, Maryland, other Mid-Atlantic states
- Developer focus on making Appalachia "thicker" may delay new expeditions
